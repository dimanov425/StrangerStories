# Stranger Stories — Product Brief

## Executive Summary

Stranger Stories is a community creative writing game where users receive a random photograph of a place and write a short story about it in 3 minutes. Other users rate the stories, and a collective storybook — an anthology written by strangers — emerges from the community over time.

The product sits at the intersection of creative gaming, micro-fiction, and community-driven content. It is not a writing tool; it is a creative game that produces a literary artifact as its output.

## The Problem

Creative writing is intimidating. The blank page, the inner critic, the pressure to be "good" — all of these prevent millions of people who *could* write from ever starting. Existing platforms (Wattpad, Medium, writing forums) cater to committed writers and reward polished, long-form work. There is no low-stakes, high-fun entry point for casual creative expression.

At the same time, people consume massive amounts of short-form content (TikTok, Instagram Stories, tweets) but have few outlets to *create* short-form narrative content. The gap between "I scroll through content" and "I create content" remains enormous for text-based creativity.

## The Solution

Stranger Stories removes every barrier to creative writing:

- **No blank page** — a photo gives you a starting point
- **No perfectionism** — 3 minutes means you can't overthink it
- **No commitment** — each story is self-contained, 300-500 words
- **No judgment pressure** — anonymous writing option, community is collaborative not competitive
- **No isolation** — your story joins a collective anthology, you're part of something bigger

The core loop is simple: **See photo → Write (3 min) → Submit → Rate others → See your story in the anthology.**

## What Makes This Different

| Dimension | Traditional Writing Platforms | Stranger Stories |
|-----------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| Prompt type | Text prompts or none | Atmospheric photographs |
| Time investment | Hours to days | 3 minutes |
| Entry barrier | High (skill, confidence) | Near zero |
| Output | Individual work | Collective anthology |
| Engagement model | Publishing/reading | Creative game loop |
| Content length | Long-form | Micro-fiction (300-500 words) |

**Unique combination:** Visual prompts + time pressure + community rating + collective anthology. No existing product combines all four.

## Who This Serves

### Primary Audience: "Curious Creatives" (18-35)
People who enjoy creative expression but don't identify as "writers." They participate in online communities, enjoy games with creative elements (Drawful, Jackbox), consume short-form content, and are drawn to novel digital experiences. They'll write their first story on a whim and stay for the community.

### Secondary Audience: "Hobbyist Writers" (25-45)
People who write occasionally — journaling, fan fiction, NaNoWriMo attempts — and want a low-pressure daily creative outlet. They appreciate the constraint as a creative exercise and value the community feedback loop.

### Tertiary Audience: "Readers & Curators" (all ages)
People who prefer reading to writing. They browse the anthology, rate stories, discover favorite authors, and share the best stories externally. They are the audience that makes the writer engagement loop work.

### Future Audience: "Educators" (teachers, workshop leaders)
Creative writing teachers who want a tool for classroom exercises. Requires private groups and content moderation guarantees. Not MVP scope.

## Success Criteria

### Launch (Month 1-3)
- 1,000 stories written
- 500 registered users
- Average session length > 5 minutes
- Story completion rate > 70% (users who start the timer finish the story)
- Photo pool: 200+ curated images

### Growth (Month 3-6)
- 10,000 stories written
- 3,000 registered users
- First weekly anthology published
- 30% of users return within 7 days
- Community-submitted photos accepted

### Scale (Month 6-12)
- 100,000 stories written
- Printed/PDF anthology available
- Multi-language support (German, English, + 2 more)
- Daily active writers: 500+

## Scope

### MVP (Phase 1)
- Random photo assignment from curated pool (200+ photos)
- 3-minute countdown writing session with auto-save and auto-submit
- Story feed with community ratings (simple star/like system)
- Basic user accounts (email or social login)
- Mobile-responsive web application
- AI content moderation (pre-publish screening)
- Simple anthology view (chronological, top-rated)

### Growth Features (Phase 2)
- Daily Challenge (same photo for everyone)
- Writing streaks and achievements
- Author profiles with story history
- Advanced anthology with themed collections
- Photo submission pipeline (community-contributed)
- Social sharing cards

### Vision Features (Phase 3)
- Weekly curated anthology releases
- Printed book partnership
- Private groups for education
- Multi-language UI and content
- Audio anthology (TTS narration)
- Interactive photo map

## Vision

Stranger Stories becomes the world's largest collective storybook — a living literary artifact written by millions of strangers, inspired by the same places, from wildly different perspectives. Every abandoned gas station, every lit window, every foggy bridge has a hundred stories waiting to be told. We give people the spark, the constraint, and the community to tell them.

The long-term vision is a new form of collaborative literature — not co-authored, but co-inspired. A library of human imagination, organized by place, written under pressure, curated by the crowd.
